Dawn breaks over Bosworth Field. You mount your horse and ride into the fray, killing all in sight. You cut down foes, carve through lines, charging toward Richmond himself. Your men fall. Your horse is slain. You fight on foot, back to the mud, bloodied but defiant. You scream: “A horse! A horse! My kingdom for a horse!” But no horse comes. Richmond’s forces surround you. You are overwhelmed, not by one sword, but many. You fall. Richard III is dead.

You seized the crown through lies, murder, and fear. You ruled alone. And you died, weapon in hand, unloved and surrounded by the ghosts of your victims. Richmond takes the crown, becomes Henry VII, and marries Elizabeth of York, uniting the warring houses. Peace returns. For now
