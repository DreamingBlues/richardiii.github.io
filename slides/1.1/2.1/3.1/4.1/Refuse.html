Buckingham stands before you, expectant. He speaks of oaths made, of lands promised. He asks for what he was due—the reward for making you king. You stare through him. “You ask much,” you say, coldly. “Too much.” He leaves with his head low… but his heart burning. Days later, word arrives: Buckingham has fled, and worse—he joins Richmond, the last Tudor, now sailing with an army to challenge your throne. You issue the order: traitor. Capture him. Execute him. But the cracks have spread. Nobles whisper. Your court grows cold. You are king—but alone.

It has come to this: The Battle of Bosworth Field. Richmond has landed. Armies gather. Your enemies unite behind him—those you betrayed, those you discarded, those who fear your crown. The night before battle, you are haunted by ghosts—Clarence, Hastings, the princes, Buckingham. All curse your name. All bless your enemy. But morning comes. And now, you must decide:

1. Fight to the bitter end, leading your men personally You will not flee. You will not hide. You are Richard III—king by will, not blood. You will win by steel, or die by it. 2. Attempt to escape and seek asylum Slip away under cover of battle. Retreat. Live to fight another day. You are too clever to die on a muddy field. Power can always be reclaimed.
