The morning mist hangs thick over Bosworth Field. The banners of York flutter beside you, but many men glance sideways, unsure if they follow a king…or a ghost. You spur your horse forward, breaking formation, cutting straight for Henry’s banner. Your guards, confused, scream and follow. Reaching Henry, his blade meets yours. It is not a long duel. You are older, More ruthless, and wihtout hesitation you strike him down. Your army surges forward, emboldened. The rebels are decimated. Your crown remains.

You rule on, with all of England fearing. In the shadows Hastings watches and Buckingham broods. Thou hast got one victory, until the sons of the dead wait to rise.
