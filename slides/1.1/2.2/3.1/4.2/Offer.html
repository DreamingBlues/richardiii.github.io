He bows, but his eyes no longer shine with loyalty. You send word to Lord Stanley, stepfather to Henry Tudor. You offer him safety, gold, and influence—should he remain neutral. He agrees publicly, but his private messages speak a different tone. Even the Bishop of Ely, whom you once slighted, receives gestures of good will. “Let old wounds close,” you say with a smile that doesn’t quite reach your eyes. To the people, you present a kinder face. You reduce taxes, proclaim days of mourning for the princes (whom you pretend are simply “missing”), and speak of peace. You’ve made the offer... but you haven’t decided whether to honor it.
